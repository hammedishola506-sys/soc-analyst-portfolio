# Humans and Systems as Attack Vectors

## Overview
This room covered how attackers exploit both human behavior and system vulnerabilities to gain access to organizations.

---

## Humans as Attack Vectors

Humans are often the weakest link in cybersecurity. Attackers exploit human psychology through:

- Phishing emails
- Social engineering
- Credential theft
- Poor password practices

### Key Takeaways
- Users can unknowingly introduce threats
- Security awareness is critical
- Policies help reduce human risk

---

## Systems as Attack Vectors

Attackers exploit vulnerabilities in systems, software, and infrastructure.

### Key Concepts
- Vulnerabilities are weaknesses in systems
- CVEs (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) identify known issues
- Example: SharePoint "ToolShell" vulnerability (CVE-2025-53770)

### Attack Methods
- Exploiting unpatched systems
- Supply chain attacks
- Remote code execution

---

## Supply Chain Attacks

Attackers compromise trusted software vendors to distribute malware.

### Example:
- SolarWinds attack

### Key Insight:
Trusted software can become a threat vector.

---

## Response to Vulnerabilities

When a vulnerability is detected:

1. Identify the vulnerability
2. Assess severity
3. Apply patches
4. Monitor systems

---

## SOC Analyst Perspective

A SOC analyst must:

- Monitor alerts and logs
- Investigate suspicious activity
- Respond quickly to vulnerabilities
- Understand both human and system risks

---

## Conclusion

Both humans and systems play a major role in cybersecurity risks. Effective defense requires a combination of user awareness, strong policies, and timely patching of vulnerabilities.
